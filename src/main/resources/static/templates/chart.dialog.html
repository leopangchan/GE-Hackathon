Street trees create a safe enjoyable walking environment for pedestrians, who benefit from cooler temperatures, less exposure to UV, protection from cars, and many more hazards. Additionally, trees have been shown to have a calming effect on people, reminding them of nature and reducing their stress.

Trees absorb carbon dioxide during photosynthesis, which helps mitigate the greenhouse gas effect. Urban trees are especially beneficial because they also keep unwanted pollutants out of the air. Also, when it rains trees absorb water in both their leaves and trunks, trapping it to later be released or evaporated. This can help mitigate runoff and preserve water for climates with infrequent rainstorms.

Street trees create a natural tunnel effect which reminds drivers to drive slower, leading to fewer accidents and safer streets. Additionally, the calming effects can reduce road rage and angry driving.

Trees have significant economic impact on their nearby area and can be a surprisingly fruitful investment. Property values for a building can increase up to 20% by creating a more pleasing environment. In addition, trees have a natural cooling effect on buildings by putting them in the shade which can save on air conditioning the building. Also, due to increased pedestrian traffic and a more friendly walking environment, research has shown that businesses have a 12% higher income if the area around them has trees.
